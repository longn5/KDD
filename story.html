How far would you go to feel anything at all?

After the passing of her mother, Karen (Jessica Lynn Skinner) moves back to her hometown on the Oregon coast. Between working in a porn shop and selling alcohol to kids at the local high school; her life is truly at a dead end. Sleeping during the day and working at night, Karen finds herself desperate for something more. One night while working, Julianne (Angela Roxanne) comes into the shop asking for “sleep tapes”. These illicit recordings of people sleeping immediately pique Karen’s interest, and a bond is formed between the two. As she delves deeper into her newfound hobby, her life begins to unravel in the most dysphoric of ways.

Karen Doesn’t Dream is neither a thriller nor a pure character study, but rather a tense and moody look at how far the search for sensation will take someone. With a bleak, eerie, and quiet style that reflects her state of mind, the heart of this film is revealed in not only what Karen feels, but in what she doesn’t.
